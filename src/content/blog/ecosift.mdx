### The Landfill Problem

Kerala's landfills are overflowing. Government initiatives for new sites keep failing. The existing waste management system relies on manual segregation at households — but events, tourist attractions, and non-compliant households generate unsorted waste that goes straight to dumps. The problem isn't processing waste — there are recycling plants ready to take it. The problem is segregating it efficiently at the source.

### The Solution: Mobile Segregation Units

EcoSift uses portable waste segregation units mounted on trucks. Each unit contains a deep learning-powered segregation module with robotic arms that identify and sort waste into four categories: plastic, metal, biodegradable, and glass. Sorted waste is compressed using hydraulic presses for efficient transport to recycling facilities.

The units are mobile by design — deployed to high-waste events, rotated between neighborhoods, or sent to clean up beaches during off-peak periods. One unit, many use cases.

A separate Plastic Segregator Module goes further: it sorts plastics by melting point using heated conveyor belts at calibrated temperatures, enabling precise recycling of different plastic grades — a capability that traditional air-blowing methods can't match.

### Where It Stands

The concept has been validated through competition wins (Dekathon runner-up with ₹25,000 prize + FABLAB Kochi internship offer, Best Startup at Space Ventura, exhibited at Keraleeyam 2023). CAD models of both the portable segregator and plastic segregator modules are complete. Proof-of-concept arm control has been tested.
