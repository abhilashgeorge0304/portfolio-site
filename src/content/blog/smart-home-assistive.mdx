### Smart Home v1

During my internship at KELTRON, I developed a smart home automation system using the Matter protocol and Google Embedded Assistant API. The concept: a unified system that manages everyday tasks — adjusting lights, controlling temperature, reordering household supplies — through voice commands and intelligent automation. "Set dinner mood" → lights dim, thermostat adjusts, music plays.

I presented this at the IET Present Around the World competition, reaching the local network finals.

### The Pivot to Assistive Technology

During a separate Assistive Technology internship, I spent time with differently-abled individuals — including a vision-impaired chess player ranked among India's highest. Understanding their daily challenges made me rethink the smart home project entirely. The same automation that was a convenience for an able-bodied person was a necessity for someone with a disability.

I adapted the system to focus on accessibility: voice-to-text conversion for vision-impaired users, automated environmental controls that reduce physical dependency, and interfaces designed for users who can't interact with traditional screens.

### What It Taught Me

Technology built for convenience is nice. Technology built for necessity changes lives. I've carried this principle into every project since — including integrating accessibility considerations into the Athena education platform.
