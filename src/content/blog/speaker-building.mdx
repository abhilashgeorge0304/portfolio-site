### The Pursuit of Neutral Sound

There's something deeply satisfying about building a speaker from scratch. Selecting the right drivers for the frequency range you want. Designing the cabinet geometry to minimize standing waves and port resonance. Calculating crossover networks so the tweeter and woofer hand off cleanly without phase issues. And then listening — hearing the result of every decision you made in the design.

I've built a full system at home. The process covers everything from cabinet design (sealed vs. ported vs. transmission line), crossover topology (Butterworth, Linkwitz-Riley), driver selection (sensitivity, Thiele-Small parameters), and the constant iteration toward neutral reproduction — where the speaker disappears and you just hear the recording.

It's engineering at its most tangible: physics you can hear.
