### The Project

In 2019, I led a team of 10 students at Christ Nagar school to build a life-sized extraterrestrial rover for the annual science exhibition. No parents were involved. We managed the budget, the design, the construction, and the logistics ourselves.

### What I Learned About Teams

The team naturally split into two groups — the intellectually talented students who thrived on design and testing, and others who wanted to contribute but weren't sure how. Instead of letting this become a divide, I made it the team's strength: the design-focused students handled technical work while others managed logistics, materials, and presentation. Everyone had ownership over something, and that's what kept the project moving.

Many mistakes happened along the way. We learned from all of them and delivered. The rover was one of the standout exhibits at the fair — not because it was technically perfect, but because a group of school students built something life-sized, end-to-end, without anyone holding their hand.

*This project taught me something I still apply at Signify: most people want to work — you just need to inspire them and make them feel involved.*
