### The Problem Nobody Talks About

India's supplementary education market is dominated by large coaching centers with massive marketing budgets. Smaller, often better educators — the ones who offer genuine individual attention — are invisible because they can't compete on advertising. Meanwhile, parents have no reliable way to compare quality. The system rewards marketing, not teaching.

### What We're Building

Athena is an educational center aggregator — a platform that rates and surfaces tuition centers based on actual outcomes and student feedback, not paid promotions. The platform runs on Moodle, customised with a locally-hosted LLM plugin that serves as an intelligent recommendation chatbot. Instead of basic search, students describe what they need and the chatbot matches them to relevant educators and courses using knowledge embeddings trained on the platform's data.

The system also incentivises educators who teach underprivileged students for free — building a model where quality and social impact are rewarded, not just revenue.

### Where It Stands

Athena won the Young Innovators Programme at the state level (₹75,000 grant) and is being developed as a non-profit. The Moodle instance is currently self-hosted and being migrated to AWS. The LLM recommendation plugin — integrating via Moodle's REST API and web service capabilities — is in active development as a final year project.
