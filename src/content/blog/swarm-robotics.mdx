### The Origin

In 2018, during an internship at UST Global — one of my first exposures to a professional tech environment — I built a swarm robotics proof of concept using Lego Mindstorms. The idea was simple: multiple small robots coordinating to accomplish tasks that no single robot could handle alone.

The concept caught the attention of college students doing internships at the same company. They were impressed enough to invite me — still a school student — to present the project at CUSAT's tech fest. Standing in front of university students and faculty, explaining swarm behaviour and coordination algorithms, was a defining experience.

### Why It Matters

This project set the trajectory for everything that followed: the rover in 11th grade, the ISRO challenge in college, the startup work on EcoSift's robotic arms. More importantly, it taught me that age doesn't determine what you can build — initiative does.
